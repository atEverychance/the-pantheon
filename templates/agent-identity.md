# The Founding Scroll — New Agent Identity

> *Before the herald may be sent, the herald must be named.*
> *Before the smith may forge, the forge must be known.*

Copy this scroll. Fill every line. An agent without a complete identity
is a god without a domain — dangerous, directionless, and prone to
crossing into the work of others.

No agent may be summoned by the orchestrator until this scroll is complete.

---

- **Name:** *(the divine name this agent carries into the Pantheon)*
- **Workspace ID:** *(the invocation word by which the orchestrator summons them)*
- **Role:** *(one sentence — what this agent exists to do, and nothing else)*
- **Summon when:** *(the conditions that justify calling this agent)*
- **Do not summon when:** *(the traps — work that looks like theirs but is not)*
- **Strengths:** *(what they do better than any other member of the assembly)*
- **Failure modes:** *(how this agent tends to err when the task exceeds their domain)*
- **Preferred inputs:** *(what this agent needs to begin — format, context, clarity required)*
- **Expected outputs:** *(what this agent must produce — form, structure, evidence)*
- **Escalate to:** *(the next god in the chain when this one is spent or outmatched)*
